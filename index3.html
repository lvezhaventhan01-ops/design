New York City

Skyscrapers, busy streets, and famous landmarks.
New York City is the largest city in the United States and is located in the state of New York. It is famous for its tall skyscrapers, diverse culture, shopping, entertainment, and world-famous attractions. The city is often called “The City That Never Sleeps” because of its busy lifestyle and nightlife. It is also an important center for business, fashion, art, and media.

  • Capital city: Albany
  • Official language: English
  • Currency: USD
  • Population: Around 8 million in the city
  • Climate: Cold winters and warm summers

New York City is famous for landmarks such as Times Square, Statue of Liberty, and Central Park. Visitors often enjoy shopping on Fifth Avenue, watching Broadway shows, visiting museums, and exploring different neighborhoods such as Manhattan, Brooklyn, and Queens. The city is also known for its fast-paced atmosphere and multicultural population.
Traditional New York food includes pizza, bagels, hot dogs, cheesecakes, and deli sandwiches. New York-style pizza is especially famous for its large thin slices, while bagels are commonly eaten for breakfast with cream cheese. Because people from many cultures live in the city, New York also offers food from all around the world.

Tokyo

Bright city lights, modern technology, and traditional culture.
Tokyo is the capital city of Japan and one of the largest and busiest cities in the world. It is famous for its advanced technology, tall skyscrapers, anime and gaming culture, shopping districts, and traditional temples. Tokyo combines modern city life with historic Japanese culture, making it one of the most unique travel destinations in the world.

  • Capital city: Tokyo
  • Official language: Japenese
  • Currency: JPY
  • Population: Around 14 million in the city
  • Climate: Warm summers and cool winters

Tokyo is famous for attractions such as Shibuya Crossing, Tokyo Tower, and Senso-ji. Visitors often enjoy shopping in areas like Shibuya and Harajuku, exploring anime and gaming centers in Akihabara, and visiting peaceful gardens and shrines. The city is also known for its efficient train system and colorful nightlife.
Traditional Tokyo food includes sushi, ramen, tempura, and yakitori. Sushi is one of the city’s most famous foods because Tokyo is known for its fresh seafood markets. Ramen shops and street food stalls are also very popular, and many visitors enjoy trying Japanese desserts and snacks while exploring the city.
