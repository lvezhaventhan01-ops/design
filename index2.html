Maldives

Clear water, tropical islands, and luxury resorts.
A tropical island country in the Indian Ocean, southwest of Sri Lanka and India. It is famous for crystal-clear water, white-sand beaches, coral reefs, and luxury overwater villas.

  • Capital city: Male
  • Official language: Dhivehi
  • Currency: MVR
  • Population: Around 500,000+
  • Climate: Tropical and warm year-round

The Maldives is also famous for its underwater world. The warm waters around the islands contain coral reefs filled with tropical fish, sea turtles, manta rays, and whale sharks. Because of this, snorkeling and scuba diving are extremely popular activities there. Many tourists also enjoy island hopping, dolphin cruises, kayaking, and other water sports.
Traditional Maldivian food often includes fish, coconut, and rice. Tuna is one of the most commonly used ingredients because fishing is very important in the country. A popular breakfast dish is mas huni, which is made with tuna, coconut, onions, and chili, usually eaten with flatbread called roshi.

Bali

A perfect mix of beaches, culture, and adventure. Bali is a tropical island in Indonesia known for its beautiful beaches, volcanic mountains, rice terraces, temples, and vibrant culture. It is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the world because of its warm climate, relaxing atmosphere, traditional arts, and luxury resorts. Bali is also famous for surfing, yoga retreats, and stunning sunsets.

  • Capital city: Denpasar
  • Official language: Indonesian
  • Currency: IDR
  • Population: Around 4.4 million
  • Climate: Tropical and warm year-round

Bali is especially famous for its natural beauty and cultural traditions. Visitors often explore temples such as Tanah Lot and Uluwatu Temple, hike volcanoes, visit waterfalls, or relax on beaches in places like Seminyak and Ubud. Surfing, snorkeling, diving, and island tours are also very popular activities.
Traditional Balinese food often includes rice, vegetables, seafood, and flavorful spices. Popular dishes include nasi goreng (fried rice), satay, and babi guling, which is roasted pork seasoned with traditional Balinese spices. Fresh tropical fruits such as mangoes, dragon fruit, and bananas are also commonly enjoyed.
