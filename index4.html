Hidden Gems

Find out some hidden gems that not many know.

Hallstatt

Mountain views, peaceful lakes, and charming village streets.
Hallstatt is a small village in Austria located beside Lake Hallstatt and surrounded by the Alps. It is famous for its colorful houses, scenic mountain views, and quiet atmosphere. The village is considered one of the most beautiful places in Europe and attracts visitors because of its fairy-tale appearance and historic charm.

  • Capital city: Vienna
  • Official language: German
  • Currency: EUR
  • Population: Around 700 people in Hallstatt
  • Climate: Cold winters and mild summers

Hallstatt is famous for its peaceful lake, boat rides, hiking trails, and alpine scenery. Visitors often explore the village streets, visit the historic salt mines, and enjoy panoramic views from the mountains. During winter, snow covers the village, making it look even more magical and attracting many photographers and travelers.
Traditional Austrian food in Hallstatt includes schnitzel, sausages, pastries, and apple strudel. Many local dishes are warm and comforting because of the cold mountain climate. Fresh fish from the lake is also popular in restaurants around the village.

Chefchaouen

Blue-painted streets, mountain scenery, and traditional Moroccan culture.
Chefchaouen is a small city in northern Morocco located in the Rif Mountains. It is famous for its blue-painted buildings and narrow streets, which create a calm and unique atmosphere. The city is one of Morocco’s most beautiful destinations and is known for its relaxing environment, local markets, and mountain views.

  • Capital city: Rabat
  • Official language: Arabic and Berber
  • Currency: MAD
  • Population: Around 40,000 people
  • Climate: Warm summers and mild winters

Chefchaouen is famous for its peaceful streets, handmade crafts, and traditional Moroccan architecture. Visitors often walk through the blue alleyways, shop in local markets, visit nearby waterfalls, and enjoy mountain hiking. The city is especially popular with photographers because of its bright blue walls and artistic atmosphere.
Traditional Moroccan food in Chefchaouen includes tagine, couscous, grilled meats, and mint tea. Many dishes are flavored with spices such as cumin, saffron, and cinnamon. Fresh bread and olives are also commonly served with meals.
